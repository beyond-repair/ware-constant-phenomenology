\section{Informational Lag and the Bullet Cluster Offset}

To resolve the mass-light separation observed in merging clusters, we introduce the retarded propagator for the $A_\mu$ field. The informational potential is no longer instantaneous:

\begin{equation}
A_\mu(x) = W \int d^4x' D_{\rm ret}(x - x') J_\mu(x')
\end{equation}

\subsection{The Retardation Time}
The characteristic lag $\Delta t_{\rm lag}$ is governed by the coherence scale $r_0(M_b)$. For cluster-scale masses ($M_b \sim 10^{15} M_\odot$), the effective $r_0$ expands to $\sim 30$ kpc. The field response delay is:
\begin{equation}
\Delta t_{\rm lag} \approx \frac{r_0(M_{\rm cluster})}{c} \approx 10^5 \, \text{yr}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Centroid Decoupling}
During high-velocity collisions ($v \sim 10^{-2} c$), the baryonic gas is subject to ram-pressure stripping, while the $A_\mu$ condensate (which couples only gravitationally/informationally) persists on its geodesic. The resulting spatial offset $\Delta x \approx v \cdot \Delta t_{\rm lag}$ reproduces the observed decoupling of the lensing peaks from the X-ray gas, providing a collisionless-like signature without DM particles.
