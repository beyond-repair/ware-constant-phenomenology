\[
\nabla^2 \Phi_{\rm info} - m_\phi^2 \Phi_{\rm info} = -4\pi G W \rho_b.
\]

For the regime \( r \ll r_0 \) (Yukawa suppression negligible), the solution is

\[
\Phi_{\rm info}(r) \approx -\frac{W G M_b}{r_0} \ln\left( \frac{r}{\lambda} \right),
\]

where the IR cutoff is physically fixed by the Proca scale:

\[
\lambda = r_0 \exp\left(-\frac{1}{2W}\right) \approx r_0 \exp(-6.25).

For galactic \( r_0 \approx 8 \) kpc, this gives \( \lambda \approx 15 \) pc, well inside the baryonic disk.

Substituting into the geodesic equation for slow-moving test particles recovers the radial acceleration law:

\[
a_r = -\frac{G M_b}{r^2} + W \frac{G M_b}{r_0 r}.
