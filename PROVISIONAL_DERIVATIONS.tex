\begin{equation}

\section{Linearized Stability Analysis}

To verify viability, we expand the action to second order in perturbations \( h_{\mu\nu} \) and \( a_\mu \) around a vacuum background (\( \bar{A}_\mu = 0 \)):

\[
S^{(2)} = \int d^4x \sqrt{-\bar{g}} \Bigg[ \frac{1}{32\pi G} h^{\mu\nu} \mathcal{E}_{\mu\nu\rho\sigma} h^{\rho\sigma} 
+ W \left( -\frac{1}{4} \bar{F}_{\mu\nu} \bar{F}^{\mu\nu} - \frac{1}{2} m_\phi^2 a_\mu a^\mu \right) \Bigg].
\]

Decomposing the vector perturbation into transverse (\( a_\mu^\perp \)) and longitudinal (\( \partial_\mu \pi \)) components:

- **Transverse Modes**: Obey \( (\Box - m_\phi^2) a_\mu^\perp = 0 \). Since \( m_\phi^2 > 0 \), the dispersion relation \( \omega^2 = k^2 + m_\phi^2 \) ensures no tachyonic instability. The kinetic term has the correct positive sign (no ghosts).

- **Longitudinal Mode**: Acquires an effective mass \( m_{\rm long}^2 = m_\phi^2 + \frac{\xi \bar{R}}{3} + \) screening corrections, remaining positive in the physical regime.

The theory is linearly stable when the effective mass is treated as density-dependent.
