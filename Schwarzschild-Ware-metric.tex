\begin{abstract}
We derive the Schwarzschild-Ware metric as the static, spherically symmetric vacuum solution to the modified Einstein field equations incorporating an informational flux term scaled by the Ware Constant $W \approx 0.08$. This metric unifies subatomic vacuum screening effects (e.g., proton radius puzzle, Lamb shift corrections) with galactic and cluster-scale gravitational anomalies without requiring dark matter. The model preserves general relativity in high-density regimes while producing an effective $1/r$ acceleration contribution at large radii. We validate the framework against the ultramassive lens LRG 3-757, reproducing the observed Einstein radius $\theta_E \approx 5.2''$ with $\sim$2.8$\times$ amplification from baryonic mass alone. Solar System compatibility is demonstrated via a negligible perturbation parameter $\eta \approx 4.8 \times 10^{-11}$.
\end{abstract}
